--- layout: post title: 'Learning Lunch: Functional Principles for OO Development' ---

This week we watched an excellent talk entitled Functional Principles for OO Development from this year's Ruby Midwest conference. Presented by the passionate and amusing Jessica Kerr, this talk took us to places not normally found in the Ruby world. 

Jessica's background as a Java OO (Object Oriented) programmer and her current work with Scala, a functional programming language, has helped her see the advantages of being able to mix these paradigms for both code clarity and performance reasons. Ruby is a language that (given some caveats) allows you to program in both a functional and OO manner, and Jessica gives some interesting examples of how this can be achieved.

Jessica also wrote a blog post about her functional optimization of some example code in the Pickaxe (a popular Ruby textbook) which complements the talk.

Aside from the excellent content of her talk, it is always good to see such a confident and engaging speaker at these events.
