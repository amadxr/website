So, have you ever read those choose your own adventure books? The ones where you have to make a decision (turn left, turn right, open the locked door which may hide a monster behind it). One of my personal favorites was The Warlock of Firetop Mountain.

Writing code, believe it or not, is a lot like defeating a warlock. The video we watched for this week's learning lunch: 'Pry - The Good Parts' by Conrad Irwin, helps when you get stuck in a coding maze.

When you're stuck in a maze, it would be extremely helpful to try a few different dead ends without having to start again from the beginning each time. According to Conrad, 60% of a developers' time is spent debugging code which isn't working quite the way it should.

Pry allows you to jump into the maze, try a few different approaches to solving a problem, and then get back to the next task.

With Pry you can "cd" into an object, show and edit the methods, inspect and alter runtime variables, re-run a selection of lines of code, look up documentation, tab complete your commands and a whole lot more. All that, with really nice syntax highlighting too! I'm not quite sure of the equivalence to navigating a maze, but rest assured, the warlock would be finished in no time if you had pry in your backpack.
